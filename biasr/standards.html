BIASR-STD Series

Scientific Standards

Official technical standards published by BIASR, analogous to ISO standards, defining the measurement systems the Republic of Binod operates on.

BIASR-STD-001Unified Binod CalendarPublished

Scope: Defines the Founding Epoch (23 April 2021, 20:00 IST), the 13-month/28-day calendar structure with three-tier leap correction, and Active Temporal Flow (ATF)-gated day advancement: one Minecraft day (20 real-world minutes of ATF-active time) equals one UBC civil day. The calendar advances only during the official 20:00–03:00 IST window and freezes completely outside it, resuming automatically the next evening. This supersedes the v1.0.1 model, in which the civil date advanced continuously via a fixed Temporal Synchronization Index independent of Active Temporal Flow.

Effective: 2026-07-02 · Version: 2.0.0 (supersedes 1.0.1)

BIASR-STD-002Astronomical Coordinate SystemPublished

Scope: Establishes the reference coordinate frame used across BIASR sky maps, observation logs, and cross-observatory data exchange.

Effective: 2026-06-05 · Version: 1.0.0

BIASR-STD-003Space Observation ProtocolPublished

Scope: Defines required metadata, calibration intervals, and logging format for observatory instruments contributing to the national research record.

Effective: 2026-06-08 · Version: 1.0.0

BIASR-STD-004Temporal Measurement StandardPublished

Scope: Defines the methodology for measuring clock drift between banked Active Temporal Flow time and real elapsed time. Formerly defined the Temporal Synchronization Index (TSI); TSI is retired under BIASR-STD-001 v2.0.0, which derives the civil calendar directly from accumulated ATF-active Minecraft days rather than a continuous Earth-time ratio.

Effective: 2026-06-01 · Version: 1.1.0
