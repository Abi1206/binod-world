BIASR-STD Series

Scientific Standards

Official technical standards published by BIASR, analogous to ISO standards, defining the measurement systems the Republic of Binod operates on.

BIASR-STD-001Unified Binod CalendarPublished

Scope: Defines the Founding Epoch (23 April 2021, 06:00 UTC), the 13-month/28-day calendar structure with three-tier leap correction, the Temporal Synchronization Index (TSI = 86,400/86,736), and the bidirectional Gregorian↔UBC conversion algorithm. The Active Temporal Flow policy governing the live observatory clock is a separate BIASR operational policy layered on top of this standard.

Effective: 2026-06-01 · Version: 1.0.1

View live reference implementation →

BIASR-STD-002Astronomical Coordinate SystemPublished

Scope: Establishes the reference coordinate frame used across BIASR sky maps, observation logs, and cross-observatory data exchange.

Effective: 2026-06-05 · Version: 1.0.0

BIASR-STD-003Space Observation ProtocolPublished

Scope: Defines required metadata, calibration intervals, and logging format for observatory instruments contributing to the national research record.

Effective: 2026-06-08 · Version: 1.0.0

BIASR-STD-004Temporal Measurement StandardPublished

Scope: Defines the Temporal Synchronization Index (TSI) and the methodology for measuring drift between Active Temporal Flow and real elapsed time.

Effective: 2026-06-01 · Version: 1.0.0
