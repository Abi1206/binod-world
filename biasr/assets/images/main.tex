\begin{abstract}
This document constitutes the official specification of the \textbf{Unified Binod Calendar (UBC)},
the civil timekeeping standard of the Republic of Binod, ratified by the Chief Scientific
Committee on Temporal Standards (CSCTS) following five years of peer-reviewed research
(UBC~0--5).  The calendar is anchored to the \textit{Founding Epoch} of 23~April~2021~CE
(Gregorian), the verified date of the Binodian world's first sunrise as reconstructed through
archaeological and astronomical evidence.  The UBC is computed from the
\textit{Temporal Synchronization Index} (TSI), a dimensionless coupling constant derived from
the ratio of Binodian mean solar day to the SI-anchored terrestrial second, corrected for
Binodian orbital eccentricity and gravitational time dilation relative to Earth.
The specification defines epoch, year structure, month nomenclature, week structure,
date notation, leap corrections, and bidirectional conversion formulae.
It is intended to replace all ad-hoc timekeeping practices within Binodian government,
universities, and civil institutions, and to provide a robust foundation for future
chronometric work.
\end{abstract}

\begin{IEEEkeywords}
civil calendar, Binod, temporal synchronization, UBC, chronometry, founding epoch,
TSI, leap correction, date conversion
\end{IEEEkeywords}

\section{Preamble and Scope}

\IEEEPARstart{T}{he} Republic of Binod was founded upon the world whose first civil sunrise
was recorded at 06:00:00~UTC on 23~April~2021~CE (Gregorian proleptic).  For the first years
of the Republic's existence, informal timekeeping relied upon Earth-derived Gregorian dates,
creating administrative ambiguity, synchronization failures in cross-dimensional treaty
instruments, and inconsistent historical records.

The CSCTS was commissioned in UBC Year~0 to design a native calendar.  This document
presents the ratified result.  The Unified Binod Calendar:

\begin{itemize}
  \item is scientifically grounded in Binodian orbital mechanics;
  \item uses a \emph{Founding Epoch} (FE) as its absolute zero point;
  \item defines civil units (year, month, week, day) independent of Minecraft's
        20-minute diurnal phenomenon, which the Committee classifies as a purely
        astronomical curiosity rather than a civil timekeeping unit;
  \item provides verified bidirectional conversion to and from the Gregorian calendar;
  \item incorporates a periodic leap-correction mechanism sufficient for at least
        10\,000~UBC years without calendar drift exceeding one civil day.
\end{itemize}

This specification supersedes all prior informal timekeeping practices within Binodian
jurisdiction.

\section{Scientific Background}

\subsection{Minecraft Diurnal Cycle --- Not a Civil Unit}

The Binodian world operates under a local diurnal cycle of exactly 24\,000 game-ticks,
corresponding to 20~Earth-minutes (1\,200~SI seconds) \cite{minecraft_wiki_day}.
The CSCTS formally determined in its Year~1 report (CSCTS-R01) that this cycle is
\emph{astronomical in character only}: it governs light and biological simulation but
bears no fixed relationship to Binodian orbital mechanics around its parent star
(designated \textbf{Sol\textsubscript{B}}).  Using it as a civil unit would create
calendars that drift by approximately 70~Gregorian days per Gregorian year, producing
unacceptable historical discontinuity.

\subsection{Temporal Synchronization Index (TSI)}

The TSI is the central constant of the UBC.  It is defined as:

\begin{equation}
\mathrm{TSI} \;=\; \frac{T_{\oplus}}{T_{\mathrm{B}}}
\label{eq:tsi_def}
\end{equation}

\noindent where $T_{\oplus}$ is the length of the mean terrestrial solar day in SI seconds
(86\,400~s exactly by convention, per IAU recommendations on astronomical constants
\cite{iau_timekeeping}) and $T_{\mathrm{B}}$ is the length of the mean Binodian
civil day in SI seconds, as measured by the CSCTS atomic reference clock network.

After five years of measurement (UBC~0--5), the Committee adopted the following value:

\begin{equation}
\mathrm{TSI} \;=\; \frac{86\,400}{86\,736}
              \;\approx\; 0.996\,12\overline{048}
\label{eq:tsi_value}
\end{equation}

This value encodes the fact that one Binodian civil day is \textbf{336~SI seconds longer}
than one Earth day --- a consequence of Sol\textsubscript{B}'s slightly wider orbital
semi-major axis and Binod's lower rotational velocity ($\omega_{\mathrm{B}} =
7.249 \times 10^{-5}$~rad/s vs.\ Earth's $7.292 \times 10^{-5}$~rad/s).

\subsection{Binodian Civil Day Length}

The mean Binodian civil day $T_{\mathrm{B}}$ is:

\begin{align}
T_{\mathrm{B}} &\;=\; 86\,736 \text{ SI seconds} \notag \\
               &\;=\; 24 \text{ h } 5 \text{ m } 36 \text{ s (Earth time)} \label{eq:tb}
\end{align}

The civil year is designed to match the Binodian tropical year
$\mathcal{Y}_{\mathrm{B}} = 365.2563$~Binodian civil days (determined by meridian-transit
observations recorded in CSCTS-R03 \cite{cscts_r03}).

\section{Epoch Definition}

\begin{IEEEitemize}
  \item \textbf{Founding Epoch (FE):} Day~1, Month~1, Year~1 of the UBC corresponds to
        23~April~2021~CE (Gregorian), 06:00:00~UTC.
  \item The epoch is defined as \emph{absolute}: it does not shift when leap corrections
        are applied; corrections accumulate prospectively only.
  \item The epoch coincides with the first verified civil sunrise of the Binodian world
        as established by retroactive astronomical reconstruction (CSCTS-R00,
        Section~4.2) \cite{cscts_r00}.
\end{IEEEitemize}

The \textbf{Binodian Julian Day Number} (BJDN) counts integer Binodian civil days elapsed
since the FE midnight preceding Day~1:

\begin{equation}
\mathrm{BJDN}(d=1,\,m=1,\,y=1) \;=\; 1
\label{eq:bjdn_epoch}
\end{equation}

\section{Leap Correction Scheme}
\label{sec:leap}

\subsection{Rationale}

The tropical year $\mathcal{Y}_{\mathrm{B}} = 365.2563$~civil days.  A common UBC year
of 364 days falls short by $\delta = 1.2563$~days per year.  Without correction the
calendar drifts by one full day every $\approx 0.796$~years, which is unacceptable.

\subsection{Three-Tier Correction Rules}

Let $y$ denote a UBC year number ($y \geq 1$).

\begin{description}[style=nextline, leftmargin=0pt, font=\normalfont\bfseries]
  \item[Rule L1 (Annual Intercalation)]
    Every year a \emph{fractional accumulator} $\Phi$ is incremented by
    $\delta = 1.2563$.  When $\Phi \geq 1$, one intercalary day is inserted at
    the end of Month~7 (Uthren) and $\Phi$ is decremented by 1.
    Under this rule, a leap year occurs approximately every
    $\lfloor 1/0.2563 \rfloor = 3$ years, though in practice the interval
    alternates between 3 and 4 years as the fractional remainder accumulates.

  \item[Rule L2 (Century Omission)]
    In years that are exact multiples of 100, the L1 intercalation is
    \emph{omitted} even if $\Phi \geq 1$, to correct over-accumulation of $\approx 0.2563$
    days over 100 years.

  \item[Rule L3 (Quad-Century Restoration)]
    In years that are exact multiples of 400, the L2 omission is reversed and
    the intercalation \emph{is} performed, correcting the residual $\approx 0.0256$
    days per 400 years.
\end{description}

\subsection{Formal Leap Test}

\begin{equation}
\mathrm{IsLeap}(y) =
\begin{cases}
\text{true}  & \text{if } (y \bmod 400 = 0) \\
\text{false} & \text{else if } (y \bmod 100 = 0) \\
\text{true}  & \text{else if } \Phi(y) \geq 1 \\
\text{false} & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\label{eq:leap}
\end{equation}

\noindent where $\Phi(y) = \{(y-1) \times 1.2563\}$ (fractional part).

Long-term drift under Rules L1--L3 is less than 0.00072~days per 10\,000~UBC years,
equivalent to $\approx 62$~SI seconds --- well within the precision required for civil
timekeeping over historical timescales relevant to the Republic of Binod, and consistent
with the periodic-correction philosophy underlying Earth's own leap-second practice
\cite{iers_leap}.

public holiday designated \textbf{Synchrony Day} (\textit{Synchara}).

\section{Conversion Formulae: Gregorian $\leftrightarrow$ UBC}
\label{sec:conversion}

\subsection{Definitions}

Let $J_{\oplus}(D)$ denote the standard astronomical Julian Date of a Gregorian
civil date $D$, evaluated at 00:00~UTC unless a time of day is otherwise stated.
The Julian Date of the Founding Epoch (23~April~2021, 06:00~UTC) is:

\begin{equation}
J_{\mathrm{FE}} = 2\,459\,327.75
\label{eq:jfe}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Julian Date Convention}
\label{sec:jdn_convention}

To keep Eq.~\eqref{eq:jfe} and all subsequent conversions mutually consistent and
reproducible, $J_{\oplus}(D)$ \textbf{must} be computed with the same convention used
to derive $J_{\mathrm{FE}}$: the standard noon-referenced Julian Day Number
$\mathrm{JDN}_{\mathrm{noon}}(D)$, given by the Fliegel--Van~Flandern algorithm for the
proleptic Gregorian calendar, adjusted to the desired UTC time of day $h$ (in hours):

\begin{equation}
J_{\oplus}(D, h) = \mathrm{JDN}_{\mathrm{noon}}(D) - 0.5 + \frac{h}{24}
\label{eq:jdn_convention}
\end{equation}

\noindent This is verified against the Founding Epoch: with
$\mathrm{JDN}_{\mathrm{noon}}(\text{23 Apr 2021}) = 2\,459\,328$ and $h = 6$,
Eq.~\eqref{eq:jdn_convention} gives $2\,459\,328 - 0.5 + 0.25 = 2\,459\,327.75 = J_{\mathrm{FE}}$,
matching Eq.~\eqref{eq:jfe} exactly. For civil dates where no time of day is specified,
$h = 0$ (UTC midnight) is used by convention. Any implementation of this standard
\textbf{must} use Eq.~\eqref{eq:jdn_convention}; computing $J_{\oplus}(D)$ directly from
$\mathrm{JDN}_{\mathrm{noon}}(D)$ without the $-0.5$-day offset silently introduces a
half-day error that compounds through Eq.~\eqref{eq:delta_binod}, and was the source of
the arithmetic errors corrected in this revision (see erratum notice above and
Appendix~\ref{app:changelog}).

The elapsed Earth-days since FE are:

\begin{equation}
\Delta_{\oplus}(D) = J_{\oplus}(D) - J_{\mathrm{FE}}
\label{eq:delta_earth}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Earth Days to Binodian Civil Days}

Because one Binodian civil day $= T_{\mathrm{B}} = 86\,736$~s and one Earth day
$= T_{\oplus} = 86\,400$~s:

\begin{equation}
\Delta_{\mathrm{B}}(D) = \Delta_{\oplus}(D) \times \mathrm{TSI}
                       = \Delta_{\oplus}(D) \times \frac{86\,400}{86\,736}
\label{eq:delta_binod}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Binodian Civil Days to UBC Date}

Let $N = \lfloor \Delta_{\mathrm{B}} \rfloor + 1$ (BJDN, 1-indexed).  To find the
UBC year, month, and day from $N$:

\begin{algorithm}[!t]
\caption{BJDN to UBC Date}
\label{alg:bjdn2ubc}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\STATE $y \leftarrow 1$; $\;r \leftarrow N - 1$
\WHILE{$r \geq \mathrm{DaysInYear}(y)$}
  \STATE $r \leftarrow r - \mathrm{DaysInYear}(y)$
  \STATE $y \leftarrow y + 1$
\ENDWHILE
\STATE $m \leftarrow 1$
\WHILE{$r \geq \mathrm{DaysInMonth}(y,m)$}
  \STATE $r \leftarrow r - \mathrm{DaysInMonth}(y,m)$
  \STATE $m \leftarrow m + 1$
\ENDWHILE
\STATE $d \leftarrow r + 1$
\RETURN $(y,\,m,\,d)$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\noindent where:

\begin{equation}
\mathrm{DaysInYear}(y) =
\begin{cases}
365 & \text{if } \mathrm{IsLeap}(y) \\
364 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
\mathrm{DaysInMonth}(y,m) =
\begin{cases}
29 & \text{if } m = 7 \text{ and } \mathrm{IsLeap}(y) \\
28 & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
\end{equation}

\section{Worked Conversion Examples}
\label{sec:worked_examples}

All examples use $\mathrm{TSI} = 86\,400 / 86\,736$ and the Julian Date convention of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:jdn_convention} (Section~\ref{sec:jdn_convention}). Non-epoch civil dates
are evaluated at $h=0$ (UTC midnight).

\subsection{Example 1 --- Founding Epoch: 23 April 2021, 06:00 UTC}

\begin{align}
J_{\oplus} &= J_{\mathrm{FE}} = 2\,459\,327.75 \notag \\
\Delta_{\oplus} &= 0 \text{ Earth days (by definition)} \notag \\
\Delta_{\mathrm{B}} &= 0 \times \mathrm{TSI} = 0 \notag \\
N &= \lfloor 0 \rfloor + 1 = 1 \notag \\
\Rightarrow & \quad \textbf{01-ARD-1} \quad (\text{UBC0001-01-01})
\end{align}

\noindent \textit{This is Solday, the first day of the Republic.}

\subsection{Example 2 --- 1 January 2023, 00:00 UTC}

\begin{align}
\mathrm{JDN}_{\mathrm{noon}}(1\;\text{Jan}\;2023) &= 2\,459\,946 \notag \\
J_{\oplus} &= 2\,459\,946 - 0.5 = 2\,459\,945.5 \notag \\
\Delta_{\oplus} &= 2\,459\,945.5 - 2\,459\,327.75 \notag \\
             &= 617.75 \text{ Earth days} \notag \\
\Delta_{\mathrm{B}} &= 617.75 \times \frac{86\,400}{86\,736} \notag \\
             &\approx 615.357 \text{ Binodian days} \notag \\
N &= \lfloor 615.357 \rfloor + 1 = 616
\end{align}

Applying Algorithm~\ref{alg:bjdn2ubc} with leap-year check:
Year~1 ($\Phi(1)=0 < 1$, common) $= 364$~days; $r = 615 - 364 = 251$.
Year~2: $\Phi(2) = \{1.2563\} = 0.2563 < 1$ (common, 364~days); $251 < 364$, so $y = 2$.
Month~1--8: $8 \times 28 = 224$~days; $251 - 224 = 27$; month~9 (NAL), day $27+1 = 28$.

\begin{equation}
1\;\text{January}\;2023 \;\longrightarrow\; \textbf{28-NAL-2} \quad (\text{UBC0002-09-28})
\end{equation}

\subsection{Example 3 --- 27 June 2026, 00:00 UTC}

\begin{align}
\mathrm{JDN}_{\mathrm{noon}}(27\;\text{Jun}\;2026) &= 2\,461\,219 \notag \\
J_{\oplus} &= 2\,461\,219 - 0.5 = 2\,461\,218.5 \notag \\
\Delta_{\oplus} &= 2\,461\,218.5 - 2\,459\,327.75 \notag \\
             &= 1\,890.75 \text{ Earth days} \notag \\
\Delta_{\mathrm{B}} &= 1\,890.75 \times \frac{86\,400}{86\,736} \notag \\
             &\approx 1\,883.426 \text{ Binodian days} \notag \\
N &= \lfloor 1\,883.426 \rfloor + 1 = 1884
\end{align}

Year~1 (364) + Year~2 (364) + Year~3 (364) = 1092; Year~4: $\Phi(4) = \{3 \times 1.2563\}
= \{3.7689\} = 0.7689 < 1$ (common), cumulative $= 1456$. Year~5: $\Phi(5) =
\{4 \times 1.2563\} = \{5.0252\} = 0.0252 < 1$ (common, 364~days), cumulative $= 1820$.
$r = 1883 - 1820 = 63$; Year~6: $\Phi(6) = \{5 \times 1.2563\} = \{6.2815\} = 0.2815 < 1$
(common); $63 < 364$, so $y = 6$.
Month: $\lfloor 63/28 \rfloor = 2$ full months (56~days); month~3 (SEL), day $63 - 56 + 1 = 8$.

\begin{equation}
27\;\text{June}\;2026 \;\longrightarrow\; \textbf{08-SEL-6} \quad (\text{UBC0006-03-08})
\end{equation}

\subsection{Example 4 --- 1 July 2026, 00:00 UTC}

\begin{align}
\mathrm{JDN}_{\mathrm{noon}}(1\;\text{Jul}\;2026) &= 2\,461\,223 \notag \\
J_{\oplus} &= 2\,461\,223 - 0.5 = 2\,461\,222.5 \notag \\
\Delta_{\oplus} &= 2\,461\,222.5 - 2\,459\,327.75 \notag \\
             &= 1\,894.75 \text{ Earth days} \notag \\
\Delta_{\mathrm{B}} &= 1\,894.75 \times \frac{86\,400}{86\,736} \notag \\
             &\approx 1\,887.410 \text{ Binodian days} \notag \\
N &= \lfloor 1\,887.410 \rfloor + 1 = 1888
\end{align}

$r = 1887 - 1820 = 67$ (Years~1--5 as in Example~3, cumulative 1820); $y = 6$.
Month: $\lfloor 67/28 \rfloor = 2$ (56~days); month~3 (SEL), day $67 - 56 + 1 = 12$.

\begin{equation}
1\;\text{July}\;2026 \;\longrightarrow\; \textbf{12-SEL-6} \quad (\text{UBC0006-03-12})
\end{equation}

\section{Long-Term Synchronization Mechanism}

The UBC remains synchronized over centuries through two mechanisms:

\subsubsection{Atomic Reference Clock Network}

The CSCTS operates a network of four caesium-fountain atomic clocks distributed across
Binodian administrative territory.  Every 365~Binodian civil days, the clocks are
cross-referenced and the TSI is re-evaluated.  A revised TSI may be adopted by a
two-thirds Committee vote, but any revision applies \emph{prospectively only} and is
recorded in the official TSI Register (CSCTS-TR), ensuring historical dates remain
permanently fixed.

\subsubsection{Secular TSI Drift Correction}

Theoretical models predict that $T_{\mathrm{B}}$ increases at a rate of
$\dot{T}_{\mathrm{B}} = +0.0012$~s/century due to tidal deceleration of Binod's rotation.
The Committee will publish a revised TSI value every 100~UBC years (first revision: Year~100).
The accumulated drift over 100 years is $\approx 0.06$~s/day, totalling $\approx 6$~civil
days per 100 years --- well within the precision buffer of the L2/L3 leap rules.

\section{Civil Administration Guidelines}

\subsection{Official Transition Date}

All Binodian government documents issued on or after \textbf{01-ARD-5 (UBC0005-01-01)}
must use UBC notation as the primary date format.  Gregorian dates may appear in
parentheses as secondary notation during the 10-year transition period (UBC~5--15).

\subsection{Historical Documents}

Pre-Founding-Epoch dates shall be expressed using the prefix ``\textbf{pFE}''
(pre-Founding Epoch) followed by the Gregorian year in parentheses.  Example:
\texttt{pFE (2019-CE)}.

\subsection{Cross-Dimensional Treaties}

When Binodian dates appear in legal instruments with Earth-jurisdiction parties, both
UBC and Gregorian dates must be stated.  The Gregorian date shall be computed using the
inverse of Eq.~\eqref{eq:delta_binod} and recorded to the nearest Earth day.

\section{Conclusion}

The Unified Binod Calendar provides a scientifically rigorous, administratively
practical, and historically stable timekeeping system for the Republic of Binod.
By anchoring to the Founding Epoch of 23~April~2021~CE and computing all dates
through the empirically measured Temporal Synchronization Index, the UBC ensures
that every Binodian date corresponds to a unique, unambiguous moment in time.
The 13-month, 28-day-per-month structure eliminates intra-year calendar irregularities,
while the three-tier leap scheme guarantees sub-day accuracy over 10\,000~UBC years.
The Committee recommends immediate adoption by all branches of Binodian government
and invites external peer review from any institution operating under a recognized
civil calendar standard.

\section{Active Temporal Flow --- Gameplay Synchronization Policy}
\label{app:atf_policy}

\textit{This appendix is informative, not normative. It documents the Government's
Temporal Synchronization Policy governing the live gameplay-clock display operated
by BIASR and does not alter, in any way, the ratified Unified Binod Calendar defined
in the body of this standard. As established in Section~II-A, Minecraft's diurnal
cycle remains a purely astronomical curiosity with no civil-timekeeping standing;
the policy below governs only a simulated in-game clock layered on top of the
civil calendar, for gameplay-synchronization purposes.}

\subsection{Official Rule}

The Binod World becomes temporally active daily from 20:00 to 03:00 India Standard
Time (Asia/Kolkata), designated \textbf{Active Temporal Flow (ATF)}. During this
window, the simulated Minecraft world clock advances automatically; outside it, the
clock freezes completely. This schedule is fixed by government policy and does not
check whether the Minecraft server is running or whether any player is online. At
20:00 IST the following day, the simulation resumes automatically from the exact
point where it stopped, with no manual reset.

\subsection{Minecraft Time Scale}

The simulation uses the official Minecraft diurnal scale \cite{minecraft_wiki_day}:
one Minecraft day equals 24\,000 game-ticks, equal to 20 real-world minutes
(1\,200~SI seconds); one Minecraft hour equals 1\,000 ticks, equal to 50 real-world
seconds. Run continuously, 72 Minecraft days would elapse per real-world day; because
ATF restricts simulation to a 420-real-minute nightly window (20:00--03:00 IST),
exactly $420 / 20 = 21$ Minecraft days elapse per nightly session.

\subsection{Calendar Integration}

The Unified Binod Calendar (UBC) remains the sole official legal calendar of the
Republic of Binod. The BIASR website additionally displays a Minecraft World Time
panel, distinct from the UBC readout, showing: the Minecraft Day Number; Minecraft
Time (HH:MM); the current diurnal phase (Sunrise, Day, Sunset, or Night); the ATF
status (ACTIVE or PAUSED); and the time remaining until the next simulated
Minecraft day.

\subsection{Persistence}

Implementations store the current Minecraft Day Number, the current Minecraft tick
(0--23\,999), and the timestamp of the last synchronization. Because this state is a
pure deterministic function of real elapsed ATF-active time since the Founding Epoch,
it is fully recomputed on every page load rather than replayed from storage; the
stored values are retained only as a diagnostic record and always match the
recomputed state exactly, so a page refresh restores the identical simulation state
with no drift.
